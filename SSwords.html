Silver & Steel Swords

I made a silver and steel sword , inspired by the Witcher 3
Using Blender for modeling, Substance Painter for Texture
and Marmoset Toolbag 3 for Render

Work time :4:00 (Silver) / 4:00 (Steel) (02.03.2020-06.03.2020)
0:30 / 1:15 Low Poly Mesh
2:40 / 1:15h High Poly Mesh
0:30 / 1:15h Texture
0:30h Render

As in the case of P2020, making this swords help me understand this kind of workflow.
The steel sword is the first one that I did and you can see that by the ammount that the low poly mesh it took me to make it. Also it was my first time sculpting so that's why the high poly for the silver sword took me that much.
The technique that I use for modeling was to have reference of the swords facing sideways so I could have a easier time trying to model them. Also I use 4 meshes, 2 low poly for game implementation and 2 high poly for texture paiting. It did this because it optimize the uses of memory in the game, by baking the high poly meshes of around 1M Tris (25 MB) on a low poly 2.8k tirs meshes (127 KB) and faking the details with a normal map.
I really enjoy making them and I plan that in the future I will make more swords, probably even a game bundle.

The skills that I use making this swords were:

  • Basic 3D Modeling. (low poly)
  • Advanced 3D Modeling. (high poly)
  • Texture knowledge. (UV Unwraping)
  • Texture Painting.
  • Node knowledge. (Asigning texture to the model)
  • PBR materials knowledge. (Roughness & Metalic workflow)
There is room for improvment? Sure it is. Some observations that I can say, refer to the fact that the handle texture on the silver sword has a patch of other color, a small mistake, but it make the sword look terible.

Did I learn something from making this model? Yes.
